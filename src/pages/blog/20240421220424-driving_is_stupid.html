Driving is stupid

There is a romance to driving. Learning feels like unlocking a new kind of freedom. It's hard to shake the feeling that driving is an inalienable right. It's the only way to conveniently reach many (most) places. To question the supremacy of the motorist is blasphemy. But this 'freedom' comes at a hidden personal and societal cost. How did we find ourselves in this 5-doored prison? What is the alternative?

There are some interesting insights to explore as we unpack our relationship with the humble car.

  1. We start by thinking about where cars came from.
  2. We then marvel at the capitalist miracle of scaling production to a consumer good.
  3. We shall despair at the insanity of modern cities, as they are designed around cars as first-class citizens.
  4. I'll reflect on my relationship with the car (AKA how I should never have been trusted behind the wheel).
  5. I'll explain how my experiences managing road trauma have shaped my views.
  6. We shall see that people cannot reliably understand the responsibilities they have when they pilot a multitone missile on the roads.
  7. We shall reflect on the above and come up with the only sensible conclusion - driving is stupid.

1. Key Claims

  1. Cars a amazingly cool (and fun!).
  2. Their ubiquitous ownership has (irreversibly?) ruined city design for the humans that live in them.
  3. The sad reality is that people do not have the fear of god that guides them to responsibly drive these metal missiles at lethal speeds.
  4. Everyone driving cars to do everyday things is completely bonkers.
